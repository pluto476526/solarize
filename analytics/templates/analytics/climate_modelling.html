Model Configuration
  • Climate Model: NASA's GISS ModelE, GEOS, or MERRA-2
  • Scenario: SSP pathways representing different future emissions
  • Time Period: Simulation years (historical or future projections)
  • Time Resolution: Temporal granularity of output data
  • Ensemble Member: Different initial conditions for uncertainty analysis
Geographic Parameters
  • Region: Geographic domain for analysis (global or regional)
  • Spatial Resolution: Grid spacing in degrees latitude/longitude
  • Bounding Box: Custom geographic domain coordinates
Climate Variables
  • Surface Temperature: Near-surface air temperature (°C)
  • Precipitation: Rainfall and snowfall (mm/day)
  • Sea Level Pressure: Atmospheric pressure at sea level (hPa)
  • Relative Humidity: Atmospheric moisture content (%)
  • Wind Speed: Near-surface wind velocity (m/s)
  • Solar Radiation: Incoming shortwave radiation (W/m²)
Simulation Parameters
  • Ocean Model: Component simulating ocean circulation and heat transport
  • Sea Ice Model: Component simulating sea ice formation and melt
  • Carbon Cycle: Interactive atmospheric CO₂ or prescribed concentrations
  • Aerosols: Atmospheric particles affecting climate (direct/indirect effects)
  • Spin-up Years: Initialization period for model equilibrium
NASA GISS ModelE
  • Atmosphere: 3D primitive equations, 40-120 vertical layers
  • Resolution: Typically 2°×2.5°, with higher resolutions available
  • Physics: Radiation, clouds, convection, boundary layer, land surface
  • Chemistry: Interactive aerosols and atmospheric chemistry
  • Coupling: Fully coupled atmosphere-ocean-sea ice-land system
SSP Scenarios
  • SSP1-2.6: Sustainability pathway (~2.6 W/m² radiative forcing)
  • SSP2-4.5: Middle-of-the-road pathway (~4.5 W/m²)
  • SSP3-7.0: Regional rivalry pathway (~7.0 W/m²)
  • SSP5-8.5: Fossil-fueled development pathway (~8.5 W/m²)
Note: NASA's climate models are complex Earth system models that simulate physical, chemical, and biological processes. Simulation times vary based on resolution and complexity, from hours to weeks for century-scale simulations.
