Detailed Pollutant Information
Particulate Matter
  • PM2.5: Fine particles ≤2.5µm - from combustion, industrial processes
  • PM10: Coarse particles ≤10µm - from dust, pollen, construction
  • Health Effects: Respiratory and cardiovascular diseases
  • Standards: WHO recommends PM2.5 < 10 µg/m³ annual mean
Gaseous Pollutants
  • O₃ (Ozone): Ground-level ozone from chemical reactions
  • NO₂ (Nitrogen Dioxide): From vehicle emissions, power plants
  • SO₂ (Sulfur Dioxide): From burning fossil fuels
  • CO (Carbon Monoxide): From incomplete combustion

AQI Calculation Method
US AQI Standard: Based on EPA guidelines
Breakpoint System: Linear interpolation between concentration breakpoints
Worst Pollutant: AQI determined by highest sub-index
Real-time: Calculated from latest measurements
Sensitive Groups
  • Children and older adults
  • People with lung diseases
  • People with heart conditions
  • Pregnant women
Note: The AQI value is determined by the pollutant with the highest concentration relative to its health-based standard.
