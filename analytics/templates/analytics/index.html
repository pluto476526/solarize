Limitations of PV Simulation

Even the best models are approximations. Here are the key limitations you should know before trusting simulation results.

Weather Data is Historical & Averaged
  • TMY datasets (PVGIS, NASA) represent typical years, not future climate
  • Resolution: 0.25°–1° grid → local microclimates (hills, lakes) not captured
  • Extreme events (dust storms, hail) not included
Model Assumptions Simplify Reality
  • Assumes uniform module temperature across array
  • Shading modeled via horizon or simple geometry — not 3D objects
  • Soiling, snow, and degradation applied as fixed percentages
Degradation & Aging Not Fully Modeled
  • Default: 0.5%/year linear — real modules vary (LID, PID, cracks)
  • Inverter failure, wiring corrosion not simulated
  • 25-year forecast assumes perfect maintenance
Real Systems Have Variability
  • Module-to-module mismatch, manufacturing tolerances (±3%)
  • Tracker accuracy, wind stow, maintenance downtime not modeled
  • Grid curtailment, export limits ignored

Your Input = Your Results

This app and pvlib do not prevent incorrect simulations. The accuracy of your results depends entirely on the parameters you provide.

User Responsibility
  • You must verify: Module specs, tilt/azimuth, losses, albedo, tracking type
  • pvlib assumes your inputs are correct — it will not warn about unrealistic values
  • Garbage in → Garbage out (GIGO) applies fully
Smart Defaults (But Check Them!)
  • Tilt: Latitude-optimized (e.g., 0° at equator)
  • Azimuth: 180° (South in N. Hemisphere)
  • Albedo: 0.2 (grass), 0.3 (concrete)
  • Soiling: 2% monthly average
  • DC/AC Ratio: 1.2 (standard)
Defaults are conservative and realistic — but always adjust for your site.

Solar Industry Glossary

AC (Alternating Current): Electrical current that reverses direction periodically. Standard grid power.
Albedo: Fraction of solar radiation reflected by the ground (0.1–0.9). Critical for bifacial gain.
Azimuth: Compass direction the PV array faces (0° = North, 180° = South).

Bifacial Module: PV panel that generates power from both sides. Gains 5–30% with reflective ground.
BOS (Balance of System): All components except modules: inverters, wiring, racking, etc.

CEC (California Energy Commission): Maintains real-world module/inverter performance database.
Clipping: Inverter limits output when DC power exceeds AC rating.
CPV (Concentrator PV): Uses lenses to focus sunlight on high-efficiency cells.

DC (Direct Current): Unidirectional current from PV modules.
DC/AC Ratio: DC capacity ÷ AC inverter capacity. Typically 1.1–1.5.
DNI (Direct Normal Irradiance): Solar radiation on surface perpendicular to sun’s rays.
DHI (Diffuse Horizontal Irradiance): Scattered sunlight on horizontal surface.

Efficiency: Ratio of electrical output to solar input. Typical: 15–23%.
Energy Yield: Total kWh produced annually per kWp installed.

GCR (Ground Coverage Ratio): Array width ÷ row spacing. Affects shading in tracking systems.
GHI (Global Horizontal Irradiance): Total solar radiation on horizontal surface (DNI + DHI).
GTI (Global Tilted Irradiance): Total irradiance on tilted plane (POA).

IAM (Incidence Angle Modifier): Loss factor due to sunlight hitting panel at an angle.
ISC (Short Circuit Current): Max current under short circuit at STC.
IV Curve: Current vs. voltage relationship of a PV cell/module.

LID (Light-Induced Degradation): Initial power loss in new modules (1–3%).
LCOE (Levelized Cost of Energy): Total lifetime cost ÷ total energy produced.

ModelChain: pvlib’s end-to-end simulation pipeline (weather → AC power).
MPPT (Maximum Power Point Tracking): Inverter algorithm to extract max power.

NOCT (Nominal Operating Cell Temperature): Cell temp at 800 W/m², 20°C ambient, 1 m/s wind.
NREL: National Renewable Energy Laboratory (USA). Creators of PVWatts, SAM.

Performance Ratio (PR): Actual yield ÷ theoretical yield. Typical: 75–85%.
POA (Plane of Array): Total irradiance on the tilted module surface.
PVGIS: EU’s Photovoltaic Geographical Information System. Provides TMY data.

SAM (System Advisor Model): NREL’s detailed PV modeling software.
STC (Standard Test Conditions): 1000 W/m², 25°C cell temp, AM 1.5 spectrum.
Spectral Mismatch: Loss due to mismatch between panel response and actual sunlight spectrum.

TMY (Typical Meteorological Year): 8760-hour weather dataset representing average conditions.
Tilt Angle: Angle of PV array from horizontal. Optimal ≈ latitude.

VOC (Open Circuit Voltage): Max voltage when no load is connected.
VMMP (Voltage at Maximum Power): Voltage at peak power point.
